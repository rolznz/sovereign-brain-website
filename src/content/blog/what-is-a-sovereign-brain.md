Most note-taking apps are rented, not owned. Your thinking lives inside someone
else's database, behind someone else's subscription, exportable only on their
terms. A **sovereign brain** flips that: your knowledge is a folder of plain
Markdown files that you control completely.

## What "sovereign" actually means

Three properties make a brain sovereign:

1. **Plain text.** Every note is a `.md` file. No proprietary format, no binary
   blob. It opens in any editor, today and in twenty years.
2. **Local-first.** The files live on your disk. You decide if and how they
   sync — git, iCloud, a USB stick, or nowhere at all.
3. **Tool-agnostic.** Obsidian, VS Code, Logseq, `vim` — the notes don't care.
   Switch tools without migrating anything.

> If you can't put your entire knowledge base on a USB drive and read it on any
> computer with a text editor, it isn't sovereign yet.

## Why it's worth the switch

- **Longevity.** Apps die; plain text doesn't.
- **No lock-in.** There's no export button to fear, because there's nothing to
  escape from.
- **Privacy by default.** Nothing leaves your machine unless you send it.
- **Agent-ready.** An `AGENTS.md` file tells AI tools how to read and extend
  your brain — so automation works *with* your notes, not around them.

## Start in five minutes

1. Grab the template — clone it or click *Use this template*.
2. Open the folder in Obsidian or VS Code.
3. Write your first note, link it, and move on with your day.

That's it. You now own your second brain. The rest is just writing.
